# Glossary

## After document

The improved version of a document. The AI studies it to learn what the final style should look like.

## Audit packet

A structured description of a Word document. It includes the document sections, paragraphs, headings, tables, formatting, and document-control elements.

## Before document

The original version of a document before improvement.

## Document control

The process used to manage approved documents, revisions, effective dates, approvals, ownership, and distribution.

## Finding

A problem or improvement opportunity found during review.

## Formatting requirement

A rule about how the Word document should look or be structured. Examples include font, headings, spacing, margins, page numbers, headers, footers, and table styles.

## Human confirmation needed

The AI is not confident enough to make the change by itself. A person should review the item.

## Master rulebook

The approved list of reusable writing, formatting, structure, and QMS rules learned from the before/after documents.

## Pair

One before document and its matching after document.

## Prompt

The instruction text copied into the AI tool.

## QMS

Quality Management System.

## Rule extraction

The process of turning observed before/after changes into reusable rules.

## Severity

The importance level of a finding:

- Critical: do not release until fixed.
- Major: significant issue; must be addressed before approval.
- Minor: should be fixed but may not block review.
- Cosmetic: appearance or low-impact style issue.

## Style

The named Word formatting applied to text, such as Title, Heading 1, Heading 2, Body Text, List Paragraph, or Table style.

## Transformation analysis

The comparison of one before document to one after document to identify what changed.
